## The Value Exchange

SuperVault curators rely on validators to post accurate, timely PPS updates on-chain. Without these updates, vaults cannot compute NAV correctly and strategies are auto-paused after `maxStaleness` (24 hours). Validators are compensated for providing this service and bear economic risk if they fail to do so honestly.

---

## Rewards

### Upkeep payments

Vault curators pay **$UP upkeep fees** to maintain active PPS oracle coverage for their vaults. These fees are collected by the protocol and distributed to the active validator set.

Reward distribution is proportional to **weighted validator stake** — validators with more $UP bonded receive a larger share of upkeep fees. All active validators that participated in a given epoch are eligible.

### Participation requirement

Rewards are contingent on consistent participation. A validator that misses rounds — whether from downtime, misconfiguration, or network issues — earns proportionally less. The exact participation threshold is set by governance.

## $UP Staking

### Bonding

To join the validator set in Phase 2, validators must bond $UP tokens. The bond:

- Acts as collateral that can be slashed for misbehavior
- Determines the validator's weight in both consensus and reward distribution
- Must be maintained above a minimum threshold to remain in the active set

### Unbonding

Unbonding is subject to a lockup period. During unbonding, the validator remains eligible for slashing for any misbehavior that occurred while bonded. After the lockup period, $UP is returned to the validator's wallet.

---

## Slashing

Slashing reduces a validator's bonded $UP as a penalty for provable misbehavior. Two conditions trigger slashing:

### Misreporting

If a validator signs a PPS report that deviates materially from the true value — or signs a report that was never agreed upon by the majority — their bond can be slashed. Slashing for misreporting requires an on-chain proof submitted by any party.

### Insufficient participation

Validators that fall below the minimum participation threshold for a sustained period are subject to partial slashing. This incentivizes operators to maintain uptime and not coast on the network's fault tolerance.

### Slashing amounts

Slashing parameters (percentage of bond, minimum participation threshold, observation period) are set by governance. The Superform team will publish these before Phase 2 launches.

## Insurance Fund

The protocol maintains an **insurance fund** seeded from a share of upkeep fees. Its purpose is to backstop depositors in the event of an oracle failure that causes material loss — for example, if a coordinated validator misbehavior causes a vault to accept incorrect redemption pricing.

The insurance fund does not cover:
- Smart contract bugs unrelated to the oracle
- Market losses from underlying yield source performance
- Losses below a minimum materiality threshold

Details on fund size, claim conditions, and governance will be published before Phase 2.

## Phase Rollout

| Phase | Mechanism | Status |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Phase 1 | Permissioned validator set, no staking or slashing | Live |
| Phase 2 | Open $UP bonding, slashing, insurance fund seeding | Planned |
| Phase 3 | Fully decentralized validator rotation via governance | Planned |

During Phase 1, validators are invited and trusted by the Superform team. Economic incentives and penalties will be introduced in Phase 2 alongside the staking contracts.

## Summary

| Mechanism | Direction | Trigger |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Upkeep fee share | Validator receives $UP | Participation in active epochs |
| Slashing — misreporting | Validator loses $UP | Provable false PPS signature |
| Slashing — downtime | Validator loses $UP | Participation below threshold |
| Insurance fund payout | Depositor receives compensation | Oracle failure causing verified loss |
