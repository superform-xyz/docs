## Overview

This page outlines an example flagship policy for curators running SuperVaults on Superform.

It is designed to read in two ways at once: as Superform's flagship policy framework, and as an illustrative example of the standard curators should use when drafting a public-facing investment policy of their own.

The objective is not to maximize quoted APY in any single week. It is to compound durable, realizable onchain yield from exposures that can be sized, monitored, explained, and exited under pressure.

Use this framework as a model for conservative flagship construction, then adapt the limits and workflows to your own mandate.

## Scope

This example policy applies to flagship SuperUSDC and SuperWETH mandates on Ethereum and Base.

| Parameter | Policy |
| --- | --- |
| Mandates in scope | Flagship SuperUSDC and SuperWETH |
| Chains in scope | Ethereum and Base |
| Cross-chain allocation | Not permitted inside a flagship mandate |
| Liquidity assumption | No reliance on cross-chain liquidity |

Each chain is managed independently. There is no blended construction across chains.

## Investment objective and risk profile

Flagship mandates should target repeatable, risk-adjusted yield with capital preservation and redemption reliability ahead of yield maximization.

As a working benchmark, flagship construction should aim for mostly organic yield from lending carry, fixed-rate carry, and other economically legible sources. Incentives may contribute to return, but they should be treated as supplemental and haircut when realization is uncertain. Purely speculative rewards, points, or unpriced optionality should not be relied on as the core of the mandate.

This is a conservative onchain mandate. That means:

- most capital belongs in battle-tested venues with clear monitoring and withdrawal paths
- concentration should be intentionally limited across protocols, curators, markets, and collateral types
- liquidity should be protected as a live operating constraint, not assumed from headline TVL
- governance, oracle, and chain dependencies should be treated as real risk factors, not background noise

## Strategy architecture

Flagship construction starts from a simple principle: most capital should sit in exposures with legible risk, repeatable monitoring, and well-understood exit paths.

Higher-yielding sleeves can exist, but only around that core and only inside hard limits.

The portfolio is divided into three sleeves.

### Anchor sleeve

The anchor sleeve is the core of flagship capital. It is defined by role in the portfolio, not by marketing claims or temporary yield leadership.

Only the following qualify for the anchor sleeve:

- Aave
- Morpho vaults curated by Gauntlet
- Morpho vaults curated by Steakhouse
- Morpho vaults curated by Karpatkey

*These named Morpho curators reflect Superform's flagship example policy, not a universal statement of curator quality across all mandates or market conditions.*

Nothing else qualifies as anchor. If an exposure sits outside that set, it is not core by definition.

### Tactical sleeve

The tactical sleeve exists to improve portfolio yield where the opportunity is understandable, measurable, and reducible.

Fluid and Gearbox are the reference tactical venues in this policy. Other non-anchor exposures may enter the mandate only if they satisfy the same flagship eligibility standard, including reputable audits, sufficient production history, clear oracle design, and a defensible exit path, but they do not become anchor simply by behaving well for a period of time.

Tactical is additive, not load-bearing. If a position materially complicates monitoring, weakens exit confidence, or becomes difficult to defend in size, it does not belong here.

### PT sleeve

The PT sleeve is permitted, but only as a capped satellite sleeve.

Vanilla PT is allowed but capped. Bespoke or synthetic PT is tactical only and must never be treated as core exposure.

PT can improve carry, but it should not become the dominant driver of flagship yield or the part of the book that determines whether the mandate still feels liquid.

## Eligible venues and flagship standard

An exposure is only eligible for flagship capital if it clears a higher bar than simple yield attractiveness.

Monitoring, simulation, and exit clarity are hard requirements.

Before sizing a position, the curator should be able to:

1. Observe it properly
2. Understand how it behaves through scenario analysis
3. Describe a credible exit path

If any of those fail, the exposure is not flagship-eligible.

In practice, flagship-eligible exposure should usually come from reputable, audited protocols with meaningful production history and operating scale. Morpho markets are the clearest flagship example. Comparable venues can qualify, but only if they meet the same standard for smart contract quality, governance quality, oracle design, liquidity, and live monitoring.

| Sleeve | Eligible exposure set | Policy treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Anchor sleeve | Aave, Morpho vaults curated by Gauntlet, Steakhouse, and Karpatkey | Core allocation sleeve |
| Tactical sleeve | Fluid, Gearbox, and other non-anchor exposures that satisfy the flagship standard | Additive yield sleeve, not load-bearing |
| PT sleeve | Vanilla PT exposure | Capped satellite sleeve |

## Allowed actions

The allowed action surface should be explicit. Flagship policy is not just about what can be owned, but what a curator is authorized to do in the normal operation of the mandate.

Within this policy, the curator may:

- allocate and rebalance across approved sleeves and eligible venues
- enter and exit approved positions
- rotate between approved anchor sleeve exposures
- rotate among approved tactical sleeve opportunities within hard caps
- add or remove individual vanilla PT positions within the PT sleeve cap
- claim rewards
- realize rewards through approved swap paths, including standard routing venues such as DEX aggregators or routers used for execution quality
- hold temporary idle balances as part of normal liquidity management

The curator may not:

- introduce cross-chain allocation within a flagship mandate
- add a new venue without a formal amendment
- treat bespoke or synthetic PT as core exposure
- rely on illiquid incentive marks as if they were fully realizable yield
- use unmodeled or weakly monitored exposures simply because they screen well on APY

## Liquidity policy

Sleeve classification and liquidity classification are separate. A position can sit in the anchor sleeve and still not offer prompt liquidity.

Every position should be tagged by current liquidity state.

| Liquidity state | Definition | Constraint |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Idle liquidity | Free assets sitting idle | Minimum 50 bps of total vault assets at all times |
| Warm liquidity | Capital that can be withdrawn with action, with high confidence that access is available in the near term | Minimum 10% |
| Idle + warm | Combined promptly accessible liquidity | Target 40% to 60% |
| Lukewarm liquidity | Withdrawable, but likely requires waiting for market conditions or other users unwinding | Monitor, no standalone cap |
| Term liquidity | Capital that is week-scale, or likely to require weeks to make liquid | Hard cap 20% |

Flagship policy should also define redemption expectations in plain language. As a reference standard, a flagship mandate should maintain a meaningful instant redemption buffer and keep weighted average withdrawal latency short under normal conditions. If those conditions deteriorate, reduce tactical and PT first, then restore liquidity before reaching for additional yield.

Flagship mandates should never imply guaranteed instant redemption in all market states. Instead, they should specify the liquidity profile they are built to support, the stressed conditions that can delay exits, and the actions the curator is authorized to take when prompt withdrawals come under pressure.

## Portfolio limits

These caps apply at all times within each flagship mandate:

| Constraint | Limit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Max per market | 20% | Applies regardless of sleeve |
| Max per curator | 50% | Concentration control across the full mandate |
| Max per smaller unique collateral asset | 10% | Applies even inside otherwise eligible venues |
| Max per protocol or counterparty | 35% | Aggregate exposure cap across the full mandate |
| Tactical sleeve hard cap | 20% | Tactical sleeve is additive, not core |
| PT sleeve hard cap | 20% | PT sleeve must remain capped |
| PT sleeve target | 10% | Target allocation, not required utilization |
| Term liquidity hard cap | 20% | Liquidity policy overrides yield chasing |

These are construction limits, not suggestions.

## Reward treatment

Displayed yield and underwritten yield are not the same thing.

Flagship reporting should distinguish among:

- organic yield that comes from lending, fixed-rate carry, or other durable economic activity
- incentivized yield that depends on token emissions or temporary programs
- speculative upside such as points or uncertain future distributions

**MORPHO rewards may be counted at par when liquidity, execution path, and realization history support that treatment.** Otherwise, they should be haircut like other rewards based on realizable liquidity and execution certainty.

In practice, that usually means a **20% to 80% haircut**, and in many cases the correct value is **zero**.

Rewards only matter to flagship construction if they can be harvested, monetized, and defended as real return. Reward realization may include swapping through approved routing venues as part of ordinary portfolio operations.

## Operating controls

This mandate assumes active portfolio control. It is not a passive basket.

Curators should continuously monitor:

- liquidity condition
- reward composition
- rate drift
- concentration by protocol, market, curator, and collateral type
- oracle dependencies and price source quality
- material governance changes that can alter withdrawal, collateral, or parameter risk

At any point, the curator should be able to explain:

- how a position exits
- what the likely frictions are
- what has to go right for current yield to be realized
- what smart contract, oracle, or governance assumptions the position depends on

If monitoring quality deteriorates, simulation stops being decision-useful, or exit mechanics become unclear, the position has failed the flagship standard even if headline APY still looks attractive.

## Amendment rules

Formal amendment is required for:

- adding a new venue to the policy
- changing the definition of the anchor sleeve or tactical sleeve
- changing the scope of assets or chains in mandate scope

Formal amendment is not required for:

- adding new PT positions within the PT sleeve
- changing reward treatment or haircut methodology
- routine portfolio rebalancing within the approved action surface

Formal amendment is required for changes to hard caps, sleeve targets, and liquidity limits.

## Excluded exposures

By design, this flagship mandate excludes:

- cross-chain allocation inside a flagship mandate
- any anchor designation beyond Aave and Morpho curators Gauntlet, Steakhouse, and Karpatkey
- unaudited or weakly monitored venues
- recursive leverage or structurally amplified exposure
- algorithmic stablecoin collateral or fragile synthetic dollar dependencies
- bespoke or synthetic PT as core allocation
- exposures whose reward economics rely on aggressive valuation of illiquid incentives
- exposures without clear monitoring, simulation, and exit discipline

## Bottom line

A flagship SuperVault should feel conservative in structure even when it is efficient in yield.

The core belongs in the anchor sleeve. Tactical belongs in a controlled satellite sleeve. The PT sleeve is allowed, but capped and watched. Liquidity is managed as a live policy, not a loose aspiration.

If an allocation cannot be monitored, simulated, and exited with confidence, it does not belong in the flagship mandate.
